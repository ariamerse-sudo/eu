What Are Secondary Schools Like in Georgia?

In Georgia, students go to school from grade 1 to grade 12. The last partâ€”grades 10 to 12â€”is called secondary education. These are the final years before students graduate and decide whether to go to university or start working.

Types of Schools in Georgia

Public Schools:

Free and run by the government. Most students go here. They teach grades 1â€“12.

Private Schools:

Paid schools with smaller classes or special programs. Some teach in English or follow international systems.

What Subjects Do Students Learn?

Core subjects:

Georgian language, math, history, biology, chemistry, physics, and foreign languages (like English or Russian).

Optional subjects:

Art, music, computer science, physical education, and civic education.

Students can choose some subjects based on what they enjoy or what they want to do in the future.

What's a Typical School Day Like?

  • School usually starts around 8:30 in the morning and ends by 3:00 in the afternoon
  • There are about 6â€“7 lessons each day, and each lesson lasts around 45 minutes
  • Students get short breaks between classes and a longer break for lunch
  • Some schools have cafeterias, but many students bring food from home

After School Activities

After classes, students can join fun activities like:

  • Sports teams (football, basketball, etc.)
  • Music or dance groups
  • Art clubs
  • Debate or science clubs

These activities help students make friends, learn new skills, and enjoy school life.

Exams and Cheating

Students used to take graduation exams, but now they only take national exams at the end of grade 12 if they want to go to university. These exams are important and taken very seriously.

To stop cheating, schools use cameras and special rules during exams. Teachers also talk to students about honesty and doing their best.

St. Matthias the Apostle Lyceum

St. Matthias the Apostle Lyceum is a private school in Georgia that teaches students from grades 1 through 12. It offers a full general education, starting from early childhood and continuing all the way to graduation. The school focuses on core academic subjects like Georgian language, English, and mathematics, helping students build strong foundations for future studies and careers.

After-School Programs

What makes this school special is its after-school program. Students have the chance to explore their talents in many creative and cultural activities. These include dance, folk dance, traditional Georgian chanting, piano, music (solfejio), and performing in a school band. Artistic students can join clubs for painting, felting, tapestry, batik, modeling, and sewing. There are also options for learning more English, playing chess, and joining sports teams like football and basketball.

A Place to Grow

The school creates a lively and supportive environment where students not only study but also grow as individuals. With so many opportunities to learn, create, and connect, St. Matthias the Apostle Lyceum is a place where education feels exciting and meaningful.
